Malala Yousafzai

Youngest Nobel Prize laureate

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She advocates for female education as the right to an education is often constrained to only males throughout Pakistan. In 2012, Malala was shot in the head by Taliban Terrorists due to her activism regarding female education, yet she is a survivor. She is an accomplished author of several novels and today she has her own fund for female education.

Ada Lovelace

The First Computer Scientist

Did you know the first computer scientist was a woman? Ada Lovelace created the first computer prgram and is creditted to be the first computer scientist to exist. Ada Lovelace was born in London, England in December of 1815. She was an English mathematician and writer. She actualy wrote the first computer program before a computer was even invented!

Audre Lorde

Writer, Civil Rights Activist

Audre Lorde described herself as “black, lesbian, mother, warrior, poet” She was born in February of 1934 and died November of 1992. Audre Lorde dedicated her entire life to social issues, specifically feminism. She is known for her passionate written pieces about feminism and racial issues. Some of her most popular pieces include “Sister Outsider” and “Your Silence Will Not Protect You.”
